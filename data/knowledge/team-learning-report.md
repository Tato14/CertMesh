# Northwind Health — Quarterly Team Learning Report (synthetic)

This report summarises certification progress at the team and track level. By
policy it contains aggregate figures only and never names individuals; groups
smaller than three learners are suppressed.

## Programme principles
- Readiness is measured against each certification's published pass threshold; a learner is "on track" when their average practice score is within ten points of that threshold.
- A team is flagged for exam risk when its average practice score sits below the pass threshold for its primary certification.
- A team is flagged as capacity-constrained when its members average fewer than six focus hours per week, because sustained certification study needs protected focus time.
- Manager views are aggregate and thresholded; they are decision-support for planning, not performance management of individuals.

## Track summary
- The technical track is the largest and is broadly on track, though the data-engineering group preparing for DP-203 is the main exam-risk area this quarter.
- The clinical track is progressing well on practice scores but is the most capacity-constrained, because clinical staff carry a heavy meeting and clinic load.
- The compliance track is small and steady, with readiness concentrated in its more experienced members.

## Recommended management actions
- For exam-risk teams, prioritise protected study time and a focused remediation plan on the weakest skills before booking exams.
- For capacity-constrained teams, lengthen the study schedule rather than increasing weekly hours, so the plan stays realistic against available focus time.
- Sequence certifications by prerequisite so learners are not scheduled onto an advanced certification before its prerequisite is complete.
