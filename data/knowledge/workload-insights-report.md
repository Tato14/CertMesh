# Northwind Health — Workload & Study-Rhythm Insights (synthetic)

This guidance explains how to schedule certification study around real work
rhythm. It uses aggregate signals — weekly meeting hours, weekly focus hours and
a preferred learning slot — and never reads calendar or message content.

## Scheduling principles
- Protected focus time is the binding constraint on study, so a schedule should allocate study only within a learner's available weekly focus hours.
- Short, frequent study sessions of about 45 minutes are more effective and more sustainable than occasional long sessions.
- Study should be scheduled in the learner's preferred slot — early morning, lunch, late afternoon or evening — because adherence is higher when sessions fit existing habits.
- When a learner's meeting load is high and focus time is low, the schedule should be spread across more weeks rather than packed into fewer.

## Reminder principles
- A reminder should arrive shortly before the learner's preferred slot, not at a fixed organisation-wide time.
- Reminders should pause during weeks with an unusually heavy meeting load so the learner is not nudged toward an unrealistic session.
- Engagement nudges are derived from aggregate rhythm signals only and carry no personal content.
